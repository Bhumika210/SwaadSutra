Following is the recipe for Momos:

Ingredients:

For Dough:
1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
A pinch of salt
Water (as needed)
For Filling:
1 cup finely chopped vegetables (cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, etc.)
1 small onion, finely chopped
2-3 cloves garlic, minced
1-inch piece of ginger, minced
1 green chili, finely chopped
2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
Salt to taste
1 tablespoon oil

Instructions:

Prepare the Dough:
1. In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour and a pinch of salt.
2. Gradually add water and knead into a smooth and pliable dough.
3. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.
Prepare the Filling:
1. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
2. Add minced garlic, ginger, and green chili. Sauté for a minute.
3. Add finely chopped onions and cook until translucent.
4. Add finely chopped vegetables and sauté until they are cooked but still crunchy.
5. Season with salt and stir in chopped coriander leaves.
6. Remove from heat and let the filling cool completely.
Shape the Momos:
1. Divide the rested dough into small equal-sized balls.
2. Roll out each ball into a thin circle using a rolling pin.
3. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle.
Fold and Seal:
1. Fold the circle over the filling to form a half-moon shape.
2. Pinch and pleat the edges of the dough to seal the momo.
3. Repeat with the remaining dough and filling.
Steam the Momos:
1. Grease a steamer basket or a perforated plate with oil.
2. Arrange the prepared momos in the steamer, leaving some space between them.
3. Steam the momos for about 10-12 minutes until the dough is cooked through and the filling is hot.
Serve:
1. Transfer the steamed momos to a serving plate.
2. Serve hot with dipping sauces like spicy chili sauce or tangy tomato chutney.
Enjoy your homemade momos, a delicious and popular snack from Tibetan cuisine!

Following is the recipe for Lo Mein:

Ingredients:

- Lo Mein noodles - Assorted vegetables (bell peppers, carrots, broccoli, etc.)
- Sliced protein (chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu)
- Soy sauce
- Sesame oil
- Garlic, minced
- Ginger, minced
- Vegetable oil
- Optional: green onions, sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions:

1. Cook Lo Mein noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
3. Add minced garlic and ginger, sauté until fragrant.
4. Add sliced protein, cook until browned and cooked through.
5. Add assorted vegetables, stir-fry until tender-crisp.
6. Add cooked noodles to the skillet or wok.
7. Pour soy sauce and sesame oil over the noodles and toss everything together until well combined.
8. Garnish with sliced green onions and sesame seeds if desired.
9. Serve hot and enjoy your delicious Lo Mein!

Following is the recipe for Mapo Tofu:

Ingredients:

- 1 block (about 400g) firm tofu, cut into cubes
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
- 2 green onions, chopped
- 2 tablespoons doubanjiang (spicy fermented bean paste)
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 teaspoon Sichuan peppercorns, crushed
- 1 cup vegetable broth
- 1 teaspoon cornstarch, mixed with 2 tablespoons water
- Salt, to taste
- Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions:

1. Heat vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.
2. Add minced garlic, ginger, and chopped green onions. Sauté until fragrant.
3. Add doubanjiang (spicy fermented bean paste) and stir-fry for a minute.
4. Add tofu cubes and gently stir to coat with the sauce.
5. Pour in soy sauce, crushed Sichuan peppercorns, and vegetable broth. Stir gently to combine.
6. Simmer for about 5-7 minutes until the tofu is heated through and the sauce has thickened slightly.
7. Stir in the cornstarch slurry to further thicken the sauce.
8. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt if needed.
9. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Enjoy your flavorful Mapo Tofu!

Following is the recipe for Vegetable Spring Rolls:

Ingredients:

- Spring roll wrappers (available in Asian markets)
- Assorted vegetables (cabbage, carrots, bean sprouts, mushrooms, etc.), julienned or finely sliced
- Soy sauce
- Sesame oil
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- Vegetable oil for frying
- Optional: cooked vermicelli noodles, cooked shrimp or tofu for added protein

Instructions:

1. In a large bowl, combine the julienned vegetables with soy sauce, sesame oil, salt, and pepper. Toss well to coat evenly.
2. If using vermicelli noodles or protein (shrimp/tofu), mix them with the vegetables.
3. Take one spring roll wrapper and place it on a clean surface with one corner facing you (diamond shape).
4. Spoon a portion of the vegetable mixture onto the bottom corner of the wrapper.
5. Fold the bottom corner over the filling, then fold in the side corners towards the center.
6. Roll up the wrapper tightly, sealing the top corner with a dab of water to secure the roll.
7. Repeat the process with the remaining wrappers and filling.
8. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or wok over medium-high heat.
9. Carefully place the spring rolls into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking.
10. Once cooked, remove the spring rolls from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
11. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce, such as sweet chili sauce or soy sauce with minced garlic and chili.

Enjoy your crispy and delicious Chinese Vegetable Spring Rolls!
