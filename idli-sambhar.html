Idli Sambhar

Ingredients:

For Idli:
2 cups idli batter
Oil or ghee for greasing idli molds
For Sambar:
1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
1 small onion, finely chopped
1 small tomato, finely chopped
1 small carrot, diced
1 small potato, diced
1/4 cup tamarind extract
2 tablespoons sambar powder
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
Few curry leaves
2 tablespoons oil
Salt, to taste

Instructions:

Prepare Idlis:
1.Grease the idli molds with oil or ghee.
2. Pour the idli batter into each mold, filling them halfway.
3. Steam the idlis in a steamer for about 10-12 minutes or until they are cooked through. Insert a toothpick into the center of an idli to check for doneness. If it comes out clean, the idlis are ready.
4. Remove the idli molds from the steamer and let them cool for a few minutes. Then, carefully remove the idlis from the molds and keep them aside.
Make Sambar:
1. Pressure cook the toor dal with enough water until soft and mushy.
2. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Let them splutter.
3. Add chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn mushy.
5. Add diced carrots and potatoes, sambar powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
6. Pour in tamarind extract and cooked toor dal. Add water as needed to achieve the desired consistency.
7. Bring the sambar to a boil and simmer for 10-15 minutes until the vegetables are cooked through and the flavors are well combined.
Enjoy your delicious idli sambar!
