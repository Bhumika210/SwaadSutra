Pani Puri

Ingredients:

For Puri (Puffed Crispy Shells):
1 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
Salt to taste
Water (as needed for dough)
Oil for frying
For Pani (Spiced Water):
1/2 cup fresh mint leaves
1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves
2-3 green chilies (adjust to taste)
1-inch piece of ginger
1 small lemon, juice extracted
1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
1 teaspoon black salt
1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
Salt to taste
2-3 cups cold water
For Filling:
Boiled and mashed potatoes
Boiled chickpeas (optional)
Tamarind chutney
Green chutney
Thin sev (fried chickpea flour noodles)
Chopped onions (optional)
Chopped coriander leaves (optional)

Instructions:

Prepare Puri (Puffed Crispy Shells):
1. In a mixing bowl, combine semolina, all-purpose flour, and salt.
2. Gradually add water and knead into a stiff dough.
3. Cover the dough and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
4. Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into small balls.
5. Roll out each ball into a thin disc using a rolling pin.
6. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium heat for frying.
7. Once the oil is hot, gently slide in the rolled dough discs in batches and fry until they puff up and turn golden brown.
8. Remove the fried puris from the oil and drain excess oil on paper towels. Let them cool.
Prepare Pani (Spiced Water):
1. In a blender, combine mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, ginger, lemon juice, roasted cumin powder, black salt, chaat masala, and salt.
2. Blend until you get a smooth paste.
3. Transfer the paste to a large bowl and add cold water. Mix well.
4. Adjust the seasoning according to your taste preference. You can add more lemon juice or salt if needed. Chill the pani in the refrigerator until serving.
Assemble Pani Puri:
1. Gently tap the top center of each puri to make a small hole without breaking it completely.
2. Stuff each puri with a little mashed potato, boiled chickpeas (if using), tamarind chutney, green chutney, chopped onions (if using), and thin sev.
3. Arrange the stuffed puris on a serving plate.
Serve:
Just before serving, pour the chilled spiced water (pani) into each puri using a spoon or a small ladle.
Serve immediately and enjoy the burst of flavors in each bite!
Enjoy your homemade Pani Puri, a delightful Indian street food snack!
