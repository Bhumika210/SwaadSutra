Jodhpuri Gulab Jamun

Ingredients:

For Gulab Jamun:
1 cup milk powder
1/4 cup all-purpose flour
1/4 teaspoon baking soda
2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
2-3 tablespoons milk (or as needed to knead the dough)
Oil or ghee for frying
For Sugar Syrup (Chasni):
1 cup sugar
1 cup water
2-3 green cardamom pods (crushed)
A few saffron strands (optional)
1 teaspoon rose water (optional)

Instructions:

Sugar Syrup Preparation:
1. In a saucepan, combine sugar, water, crushed cardamom pods, and saffron strands (if using).
2. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat, stirring occasionally until the sugar dissolves completely.
3. Let it simmer for 5-7 minutes until the syrup slightly thickens.
4. Turn off the heat and add rose water (if using). Keep the syrup warm.
Gulab Jamun Preparation:
1. In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda.
2. Add ghee and mix well to form a crumbly mixture.
3. Gradually add milk, a little at a time, and knead into a soft and smooth dough. The dough should be slightly sticky.
4. Divide the dough into small portions and roll them into smooth balls without any cracks.
5. Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan over low-medium heat for frying.
6. Once the oil/ghee is hot, gently slide the prepared dough balls into the oil.
7. Fry the gulab jamuns on low heat, stirring occasionally, until they turn golden brown and evenly cooked.
8. Remove the fried gulab jamuns from the oil and drain excess oil by placing them on paper towels briefly.
Soaking Gulab Jamuns:
1. While the gulab jamuns are still warm, gently drop them into the warm sugar syrup.
2. Let them soak in the syrup for at least 1-2 hours to absorb the sweetness and flavors.
3. Serve the Jodhpuri Gulab Jamun warm or at room temperature, garnished with chopped nuts if desired.
Enjoy the rich and indulgent sweetness of this royal dessert!
